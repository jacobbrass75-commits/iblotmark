ELD Compliance and Why Your Mount Matters

Since the FMCSA mandate took full effect, every commercial motor vehicle must have a functioning ELD visible to the driver. The device (usually a tablet or dedicated ELD unit) must be mounted securely enough that it does not become a projectile during hard braking, and positioned so the driver can view hours-of-service data without significant distraction.

A loose tablet on the dash does not meet these requirements practically, even if it is technically running ELD software. Inspectors look for secure mounting. Insurance companies look for proper device placement. And from a safety perspective, a tablet sliding across the dash during an emergency stop is a real hazard.

The right mount keeps your ELD device fixed in position, angled for easy viewing, and out of the way of critical sightlines. It also needs to survive the vibration, temperature swings, and daily use that commercial trucking demands.

What to Look for in a Truck ELD Mount

Holding force: Commercial trucks generate more vibration than passenger vehicles. The mount must hold a 1 to 2 pound tablet securely over hours of highway driving, rough roads, and engine idle vibration. Heavy-duty suction, bolt-on bases, or strong clamps are the way to go. Avoid consumer-grade suction cups.

Arm reach and adjustability: Truck cabs vary widely in layout. A mount with a 6-inch articulating arm gives you the flexibility to position the tablet where it is visible without blocking mirrors or critical gauges. 360-degree rotation is important for switching between landscape and portrait orientation.

Temperature tolerance: Truck cabs experience extreme temperatures, from below freezing in winter to 140+ degrees when parked in summer sun. Suction cups can lose grip in extreme cold. Metal arms hold up better than plastic in heat. Check the mount's rated temperature range.

Ease of transfer: Owner-operators who switch trucks, or fleets that rotate drivers between vehicles, benefit from mounts that install and remove quickly. Suction mounts offer the fastest transfer. Bolt-on mounts are permanent but require reinstallation for each truck.

Cable access: The tablet needs to stay charged. Make sure the mount's holder does not block the charging port. Most universal holders (including iBOLT's) leave the bottom edge accessible for cables.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a special mount for ELD compliance?

There is no FMCSA-certified "ELD mount." The requirement is that the ELD device is mounted in a fixed, visible position in the cab. Any secure mount that holds the device firmly and positions it where the driver can view it meets the practical requirement. During roadside inspections, officers look for devices that are properly secured, not sitting loose on the dash or in a driver's lap.

Will a suction cup mount hold in extreme heat or cold?

Quality suction cups (like the iBOLT IncrediBOLT's twist-lock design) hold well across a wide temperature range. Extreme cold (below 0F) can reduce suction grip, so if you park overnight in very cold climates, check the seal in the morning. The Triple Suction model provides extra security in temperature extremes. For maximum permanence regardless of temperature, a drill-base or no-drill bolt-on mount is the best option.

What tablet size is best for ELD use in trucks?

Most drivers find 8 to 10 inches ideal. An 8-inch tablet is compact enough to mount without blocking sightlines and large enough to read ELD logs and navigation. 10-inch tablets provide more screen real estate for split-screen navigation and ELD, but they require more mounting space. The iBOLT IncrediBOLT 360 accommodates any tablet from 7 to 10 inches.

How do I keep my tablet charged while mounted in the truck?

Most drivers use a 12V cigarette lighter USB adapter or hardwire a USB outlet to the truck's electrical system. Route the charging cable along the windshield frame or dash to keep it tidy. The iBOLT mount holders leave the tablet's charging port accessible. For all-day use with GPS and ELD running simultaneously, a 2.4A or higher USB port is recommended to charge faster than the apps drain the battery.

Can I use the same mount in different trucks?

Suction cup mounts (like both iBOLT models in this comparison) transfer between trucks in under a minute. Simply release the suction, move to the new truck, and reattach. This is a significant advantage for owner-operators who lease trucks or for fleets that rotate drivers. Bolt-on and no-drill mounts are semi-permanent and require reinstallation for each vehicle.
