What to Look for in a Forklift Tablet Mount

Vibration resistance: Forklifts generate constant vibration. Mounts designed for passenger vehicles will loosen, rattle, and eventually fail. Look for industrial-rated construction with reinforced pivot points.

Mounting compatibility: Most forklifts have a 38mm (1.5") post or tube available for accessories. Confirm your forklift has this before ordering. If not, a seat rail mount or magnetic mount provides alternatives.

Security: In multi-shift operations, keyed locks prevent tablets from walking off. This matters especially in facilities with temporary or contract workers.

Modularity: When you need to add a barcode scanner mount, upgrade to a larger tablet, or move the setup to a different forklift model, a modular system lets you swap individual components rather than replacing the entire mount.

Total cost of deployment: Multiply the per-unit cost by the number of forklifts in your fleet. The difference between $110 and $400 per unit adds up quickly across 20, 50, or 100 forklifts.

Frequently Asked Questions

Will a forklift mount damage my tablet from vibration?

A properly designed forklift mount actually protects your tablet by absorbing and dampening vibration rather than transmitting it directly. Cheap mounts with rigid plastic construction can make vibration worse. The iBOLT Bizmount and RAM Tab-Tite both use vibration-dampening design to protect devices. Regardless of mount, using a rugged tablet case adds an extra layer of protection.

Can I charge my tablet while it is mounted on the forklift?

Yes, but the charging solution is typically separate from the mount itself. Most warehouse operators run a USB cable from the forklift's battery (via a 12V-to-USB adapter) to the tablet. iBOLT and RAM both offer compatible power accessories. Zebra docks include integrated charging, but at a significantly higher price point.

What size tablet works best on a forklift?

The most common tablet size for forklift use is 8 to 10 inches. An 8-inch tablet (like the Samsung Galaxy Tab A) provides a good balance of screen visibility and compact size that does not obstruct the operator's view. 10-inch tablets are better for detailed inventory interfaces but take up more space in the cab. All iBOLT forklift mounts accommodate 7" to 10" tablets.

How long does it take to install a forklift tablet mount?

The iBOLT Bizmount installs in about 10 to 15 minutes using the 38mm post mount. RAM Mount setups can take longer due to assembling multiple components (base, arm, ball, cradle). Magnetic mounts like the iBOLT MagDock 360 install in under 5 minutes.

Are iBOLT forklift mounts compatible with RAM parts?

iBOLT's ball system uses 25mm (1-inch) balls, which is the same as RAM's "B" size. This means many iBOLT and RAM components can be cross-used if needed. iBOLT also sells ball adapters that bridge between different sizes, giving you flexibility if you have existing RAM hardware in your fleet.
