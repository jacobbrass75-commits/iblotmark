iBOLT Confirms Universal Mounting Solutions for Samsung Galaxy Tab A9 and S9 Series

Published February 20, 2026

iBOLT has confirmed full compatibility between its TabDock universal tablet holder system and Samsung's latest Galaxy Tab lineup, including the Galaxy Tab A9, A9+, S9, S9+, S9 FE, and S9 FE+. Whether deployed in a commercial fleet, mounted on a warehouse forklift, or installed at a restaurant POS station, iBOLT's TabDock holders securely grip each of these Samsung tablets with or without a protective case.

Confirmed Compatible Devices

iBOLT's engineering team has tested and confirmed the following Samsung Galaxy Tab models fit within the TabDock universal holder system:

  • Samsung Galaxy Tab A9 (8.7" display, 211.5 x 124.7 x 8.0 mm)
  • Samsung Galaxy Tab A9+ (11.0" display, 257.1 x 168.7 x 6.9 mm)
  • Samsung Galaxy Tab S9 (11.0" display, 254.3 x 165.8 x 5.9 mm)
  • Samsung Galaxy Tab S9+ (12.4" display, 285.4 x 185.4 x 5.7 mm)
  • Samsung Galaxy Tab S9 FE (10.9" display, 254.3 x 165.8 x 6.5 mm)
  • Samsung Galaxy Tab S9 FE+ (12.4" display, 285.4 x 185.4 x 6.5 mm)

All models listed above fit securely in the iBOLT TabDock holder when used bare or with standard-thickness protective cases (up to 2mm case thickness on each side). Tablets with rugged cases thicker than 2mm per side may require the TabDock XL holder variant.

The TabDock System Explained

iBOLT's TabDock is a universal spring-loaded tablet holder that adjusts to grip tablets ranging from 7" to 10" (standard TabDock) or up to 12.4" (TabDock XL). The holder uses padded grip arms that compress against the tablet's edges, holding the device firmly without blocking ports, buttons, or the camera. This universal approach means a single TabDock holder works across Samsung, Apple, Lenovo, and other tablet brands, giving fleet managers and operations teams the flexibility to standardize their mounting hardware even when their tablet inventory includes multiple brands and models.

The TabDock holder is one component of a complete iBOLT mounting solution. It attaches to any iBOLT base via industry-standard ball connections (available in 25mm B-size and 38mm C-size), creating a modular system where the holder, arm, and base can each be swapped independently.

Why TabDock Over Device-Specific Holders

Samsung releases new Galaxy Tab models frequently. A device-specific cradle designed for the Galaxy Tab S8 may not fit the S9 without modification. iBOLT's universal TabDock approach eliminates this upgrade cycle problem. When a fleet or warehouse transitions from the Galaxy Tab A8 to the A9, or from the S8 to the S9, the same TabDock holder accommodates the new device without any hardware changes. This saves organizations the cost and downtime of replacing mounting hardware every time they refresh their tablet inventory.

iBOLT's modular design means the TabDock holder is just one interchangeable piece of a larger system. The same 25mm or 38mm ball connection that holds a TabDock today can accept an iBOLT phone holder, barcode scanner mount, or custom bracket tomorrow. With over 300 parts in the iBOLT ecosystem, the possibilities for reconfiguration are extensive.
