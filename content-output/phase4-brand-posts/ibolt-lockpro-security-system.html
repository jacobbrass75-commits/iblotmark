How LockPro Works

The LockPro system uses a keyed locking mechanism built into the tablet holder itself. Once a tablet is placed in the holder and the lock is engaged, the device cannot be removed without the matching key. The holder grips the tablet's edges firmly and the lock prevents the grip arms from being opened, creating a physical barrier that deters opportunistic theft and prevents accidental removal by unauthorized personnel.

Each LockPro mount ships with two keys, and replacement keys can be ordered from iBOLT. For organizations deploying multiple LockPro mounts, iBOLT can provide keyed-alike configurations so that a single master key works across all units in a location.

The LockPro holder is a universal design that fits tablets from 7" to 10.5", including the iPad 10th generation, Samsung Galaxy Tab A and S series, and other commonly deployed commercial tablets. Tablets can be mounted with or without standard-thickness protective cases.

Why Physical Security Still Matters

Software-based security (MDM, remote wipe, device tracking) is essential, but it addresses theft after the fact. Physical locking prevents the theft from happening in the first place. A locked tablet is a visible deterrent: potential thieves can see that the device is secured and move on. For organizations that have experienced tablet theft, the LockPro system provides immediate, tangible protection that does not depend on software, cellular connectivity, or battery life.

Physical security also prevents a subtler problem: unauthorized removal by employees or well-meaning staff who "borrow" a tablet for another task and fail to return it. In a warehouse with 20 mounted tablets across various workstations, even a single missing device can disrupt operations. The LockPro system ensures tablets stay at their assigned stations unless a manager with a key intentionally moves them.
