iBOLT Mounts Supports ELD Compliance for America's Commercial Fleets

Published January 28, 2026

The FMCSA's Electronic Logging Device mandate requires every commercial motor vehicle operating in interstate commerce to use a certified ELD for recording hours of service. For the hundreds of thousands of owner-operators and fleet managers across the country, that means a tablet or dedicated ELD device needs to be mounted securely in the cab, visible to the driver, and accessible for inspection. iBOLT Mounts builds purpose-built commercial vehicle mounting solutions designed specifically for this requirement.

ELD Compliance Starts with Proper Mounting

An ELD device that is loose on the seat, wedged in a cupholder, or balanced on the dash does not meet the spirit of the mandate and creates a genuine safety hazard. During a roadside inspection, the officer needs to view the driver's hours-of-service data clearly on the screen. During normal driving, the driver needs to glance at ELD status without taking their eyes off the road for extended periods. Proper mounting solves both problems.

iBOLT's commercial vehicle mounts position tablets at an angle and height that keeps ELD data visible without obstructing the driver's field of vision. Every mount is adjustable for 360-degree rotation and multi-angle tilt, so drivers in different cab configurations (day cabs, sleepers, straight trucks, and vans) can all find the right position.

Compatible with Major ELD Platforms

iBOLT's TabDock universal holders and phone mounts are compatible with the tablets and devices used by the most widely adopted ELD platforms in the industry:

  • Motive (formerly KeepTruckin): Compatible with Motive Vehicle Gateway paired with Samsung Galaxy Tab, iPad, or Motive's own driver tablet
  • Samsara: Compatible with Samsara's CM32 gateway paired with driver-facing tablets and phones
  • ELD Rider, BigRoad, and Geotab: Compatible with the Android and iOS tablets and phones used across these platforms

Because iBOLT uses a universal spring-loaded holder system rather than device-specific cradles, fleet managers do not need to purchase new mounting hardware every time they upgrade tablets or switch ELD providers. The same TabDock holder that grips a Samsung Galaxy Tab A8 today will grip the Tab A9 tomorrow without modification.

Purpose-Built for Commercial Vehicles

Consumer phone and tablet mounts are designed for the relatively gentle environment of a passenger car. Commercial vehicles present a fundamentally different set of challenges: sustained highway vibration, air brakes and engine rumble, rough dock approaches, extreme cabin temperatures, and constant use by multiple drivers across shifts.

iBOLT's commercial vehicle mounts use materials and engineering decisions that address each of these challenges. Heavy-gauge aluminum instead of plastic. Powder-coated finishes instead of paint. Steel hardware instead of adhesive. Industrial suction cups instead of consumer-grade stick-on pads. These are purpose-built tools for professional drivers.
