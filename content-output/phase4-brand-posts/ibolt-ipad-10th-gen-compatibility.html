iBOLT Confirms Universal Mounting Solutions for Apple iPad 10th Generation and iPad Air M2

Published February 28, 2026

iBOLT has completed fitment testing and confirmed full compatibility between its TabDock universal tablet holder system and Apple's current iPad lineup, including the iPad 10th generation, iPad Air M2 (11" and 13"), and iPad Pro 11" (M4). All three devices fit securely in iBOLT's standard TabDock holders, both bare and with standard-thickness protective cases, across every mounting configuration in the iBOLT ecosystem.

Confirmed Compatible Apple Devices

  • iPad 10th Generation (10.9" display, 248.6 x 179.5 x 7.0 mm)
  • iPad Air M2 11" (11.0" display, 247.6 x 178.5 x 6.1 mm)
  • iPad Air M2 13" (13.0" display, 281.6 x 214.9 x 6.1 mm, fits TabDock XL)
  • iPad Pro 11" M4 (11.0" display, 249.7 x 177.5 x 5.3 mm)

The iPad 10th generation and iPad Air M2 11" share nearly identical physical dimensions, meaning any iBOLT mount that fits one will fit the other without adjustment. The iPad Pro 11" M4 is marginally thinner but falls within the same grip range. The larger iPad Air M2 13" fits iBOLT's TabDock XL holder, which accommodates tablets up to 13" diagonal.

All models listed are compatible when used with Apple's standard Smart Cover, Smart Folio, or third-party cases up to 2mm thickness per side. Tablets in ruggedized cases (OtterBox Defender, Griffin Survivor, and similar) should use the TabDock XL variant to accommodate the additional bulk.

Why iPad Compatibility Matters for Commercial Deployments

Apple's iPad remains the dominant tablet in many commercial environments. Restaurants running Toast, Square, and Clover frequently deploy iPads at the register. Field service companies equip technicians with iPads for work order management. Real estate agencies, healthcare providers, and educational institutions all use iPads as their primary tablet platform.

When these organizations deploy iPads in mounted configurations (POS stations, vehicle mounts, wall-mounted kiosks), they need mounting hardware that fits reliably and will continue to fit when Apple releases the next generation. iBOLT's universal TabDock approach eliminates the compatibility uncertainty that comes with device-specific cradles.

Universal TabDock vs. Device-Specific Cradles

Some mounting manufacturers offer device-specific cradles molded to the exact dimensions of a particular iPad model. While these provide a precise fit, they create a practical problem: every time Apple releases a new iPad with slightly different dimensions (even a fraction of a millimeter), the cradle becomes obsolete. Organizations then face the choice of replacing every cradle across their deployment or continuing to use outdated iPads.

iBOLT's TabDock system avoids this cycle entirely. The spring-loaded grip arms accommodate a range of tablet sizes, so the same holder that fits today's iPad 10th generation will fit whatever Apple releases next, as long as the screen size stays within the 7" to 10" range (or up to 13" with the TabDock XL). This forward compatibility saves organizations the recurring cost of replacing mounting hardware with every device refresh.
