iBOLT Tablet Tower System Featured at National Restaurant Association Show 2026

Published March 20, 2026

iBOLT will be showcasing its full restaurant technology mounting lineup at the National Restaurant Association Show 2026, held May 17 through 20 at McCormick Place in Chicago. The centerpiece of iBOLT's exhibit is the Tablet Tower system, a modular multi-tablet mounting platform designed for restaurants managing POS terminals, third-party delivery apps, and kitchen display screens from a single counter footprint.

What iBOLT Is Showing at NRA 2026

The iBOLT booth will feature live demonstrations of three core product lines built for the restaurant industry:

Quad Tablet Tower Stand

The iBOLT Quad Tablet Tower Stand ($149.95) holds up to four tablets in a compact freestanding tower, giving restaurant staff instant, organized access to Toast, Square, DoorDash, UberEats, Grubhub, and other platforms running on dedicated screens. The tower's weighted steel base keeps it stable on counters, host stands, and expo lines, while each tablet holder adjusts independently for viewing angle.

At the show, iBOLT will demonstrate the Quad Tower configured with four live tablets running simulated POS and delivery platform interfaces, so attendees can see how the system handles a realistic multi-tablet workflow.

Tablet Tower Wall Mount

The iBOLT Tablet Tower Wall Mount takes the multi-tablet concept off the counter and onto the wall. This configuration holds three tablets in a vertical stack mounted directly to a wall or partition, freeing up every square inch of counter space. It is particularly popular in quick-service restaurants and food trucks where counter real estate is at a premium.

The wall mount version uses the same TabDock universal holders as the freestanding tower, so restaurants can mix and match configurations across locations. Tablets move between the wall mount and freestanding tower without any hardware changes.

LockPro POS Mounting

iBOLT will also feature its LockPro line of locking tablet mounts, designed for customer-facing POS terminals where security is a concern. The LockPro system uses a key-lock mechanism to prevent tablet removal, making it ideal for self-service kiosks, checkout stations, and any installation where an unattended tablet needs to stay put. Show attendees can test the lock mechanism and see how the LockPro system integrates with standard POS hardware.

The Multi-Tablet Restaurant Challenge

Restaurant operators at the 2025 NRA Show consistently identified tablet management as a growing pain point. As more restaurants adopt multiple technology platforms (POS, online ordering, delivery aggregation, loyalty programs, kitchen display), the number of tablets per location has increased dramatically. A single restaurant may run four to six tablets simultaneously, and without proper mounting, those devices create cluttered, disorganized workstations that slow down service.

iBOLT's Tablet Tower system was designed in direct response to this trend. Rather than offering one tablet mount and asking restaurants to buy multiples, iBOLT engineered a single platform that holds multiple devices in an organized, space-efficient arrangement. The result is cleaner workstations, faster order processing, and fewer dropped or damaged tablets.

Industrial-Grade Construction for Restaurant Environments

Restaurants are tough on equipment. Between heat, grease, water splashes, and the sheer volume of hands touching every surface, mounting hardware in a restaurant needs to be built to last. iBOLT's restaurant products use the same heavy-gauge steel and aluminum construction found across their warehouse and fleet product lines. Powder-coated finishes resist corrosion from kitchen moisture and cleaning chemicals, and all-metal hardware ensures the mounts do not degrade or become brittle over time.

The Quad Tablet Tower's weighted base uses a steel plate heavy enough to keep the tower stable even when staff are rapidly interacting with mounted tablets, accepting delivery orders, and processing payments at speed. No clamping to the counter edge is required, so the tower can be repositioned during service as needed.

Modular Design Supports Changing Technology

One of iBOLT's key messages at NRA 2026 is the value of modularity in restaurant technology mounting. iBOLT's system uses industry-standard ball connections (25mm B-size) and interchangeable holders, so restaurants can adapt their mounting setup as their technology evolves. Switching from Samsung tablets to iPads? The same TabDock holder fits both. Adding a fifth delivery platform? Add another holder to the tower. Upgrading to a secured POS terminal? Swap in a LockPro holder on the same base.

This modularity is supported by iBOLT's catalog of over 300 interchangeable parts, all cross-compatible with RAM and other industry-standard mounting systems. Restaurants that already have RAM hardware installed can integrate iBOLT holders and accessories directly into their existing infrastructure.
