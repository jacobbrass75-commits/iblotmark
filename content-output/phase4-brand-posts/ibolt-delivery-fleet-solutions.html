The Delivery Driver's Mounting Problem

Delivery driving is uniquely hard on phone mounts. Drivers make dozens of stops per shift, constantly interacting with their mounted device to accept orders, follow navigation, confirm pickups, and complete deliveries. The mount gets bumped, jostled, and adjusted repeatedly throughout the day. In summer, the phone bakes in direct sunlight on the windshield. In winter, temperature swings from a cold vehicle to a heated cabin stress adhesive mounts and plastic components.

Consumer phone mounts from the auto parts store are not designed for this level of use. They are built for a commuter who mounts their phone once in the morning and removes it at night. For a delivery driver making 30 or more stops per shift, the mount needs to be stronger, more adjustable, and more durable than anything designed for occasional use.

Multi-App Drivers Need Reliable Mounting

Many independent delivery drivers run multiple apps simultaneously: DoorDash, UberEats, Grubhub, Instacart, and Amazon Flex. Switching between apps while driving requires a mount that holds the phone steady at a glanceable angle. A phone that slides around, tilts under its own weight, or vibrates loose during a turn is not just inconvenient; it is a safety hazard.

iBOLT mounts are engineered for the kind of repeated interaction that multi-app drivers demand. The grip arms hold the phone firmly during navigation, but the one-handed release mechanism lets drivers remove the phone quickly for walk-up deliveries to apartment buildings or office lobbies. When the driver returns to the vehicle, the phone snaps back into the same position without readjustment.
