The modern restaurant runs on tablets. Between Toast POS terminals at the front counter, delivery order management tablets for DoorDash, UberEats, and Grubhub, and kitchen display screens in the back of house, a single location can easily require four or more mounted devices operating simultaneously. iBOLT Mounts has built an entire ecosystem of restaurant-grade tablet mounting solutions designed to keep every screen secure, accessible, and positioned exactly where staff need them.

Multi-Tablet Restaurants Need Purpose-Built Mounting

Third-party delivery platforms have fundamentally changed how restaurants operate. A busy location juggling Toast POS for in-house orders alongside three separate delivery tablets faces a real logistical challenge: where do all those screens go? Duct-taping a tablet to the wall or propping one against the register is not a solution. It creates clutter, slows down service, and risks expensive hardware damage.

iBOLT's restaurant mounting solutions were designed from the ground up for this exact scenario. These are not repurposed consumer phone holders. They are industrial-grade, heavy-gauge steel and aluminum systems built to withstand the heat, grease, and constant activity of a commercial kitchen environment.

Secure POS Stations with LockPro

For the primary Toast POS terminal, security matters as much as accessibility. The iBOLT LockPro Drill Base POS Mount ($139.95) provides a permanent, key-locking tablet station that bolts directly to the counter. The LockPro system holds the tablet firmly in place, preventing both accidental drops and intentional theft. Staff can still adjust the viewing angle, but the tablet stays put.

The LockPro's drill-base design is ideal for restaurants that want a clean, professional look at the register. Once installed, it provides a stable platform that customers can use for self-checkout or tip selection without the wobble or instability of cheaper stands.
