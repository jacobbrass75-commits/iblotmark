iBOLT Launches Next-Generation Industrial Forklift Mounting System

Published March 1, 2026

iBOLT has launched its next-generation industrial forklift mounting system, a complete hardware lineup purpose-built for the daily demands of warehouse, distribution center, and manufacturing floor environments. The new system centers on the Dock'n Lock Bizmount Forklift Mount, paired with iBOLT's MagDock 360 magnetic tablet holder and XL Barcode Scanner Holder, giving warehouse operations teams a unified mounting platform for every device their workers carry.

Built for Warehouses, Not Adapted from Consumer Products

Most tablet mounts on the market started life as consumer products. They were designed for someone mounting a phone in a sedan, then marketed to warehouses with minor modifications. iBOLT took the opposite approach. The new forklift mounting system was engineered from the first prototype specifically for industrial environments: constant vibration, temperature swings, dust, moisture, and the inevitable impacts that come with daily forklift operation.

Every component in the system uses heavy-gauge steel mounting plates, precision-machined aluminum arms, and powder-coated finishes that resist corrosion and wear. The 38mm (C-size) ball connections provide a larger contact surface than standard consumer mounts, delivering greater stability and load-bearing capacity when holding tablets and scanners on moving equipment.

Dock'n Lock Bizmount Forklift Mount

The iBOLT Dock'n Lock Bizmount Forklift Mount ($110.00) is the foundation of the new system. It features a heavy-duty steel mounting plate that bolts directly to the forklift's overhead guard, mast, or dash using standard hardware. The 38mm C-size ball and socket joint provides 360-degree rotation and multi-angle tilt, so operators can position their tablet exactly where they need it for scanning, picking, or inventory lookups.

Key specifications:

  • Heavy-gauge steel mounting plate with powder-coated finish
  • 38mm (C-size) ball connection for maximum stability
  • 360-degree rotation with locking tension adjustment
  • Compatible with all iBOLT TabDock holders (7" to 12.4" tablets)
  • Vibration-dampening design tested in active warehouse environments
  • Cross-compatible with RAM C-size and other industry-standard 38mm components

MagDock 360: Magnetic Mounting for Flexible Deployments

Not every warehouse device needs a permanent bolt-on installation. The iBOLT MagDock 360 ($76.95) uses industrial-strength magnets to attach directly to any ferrous metal surface on a forklift, pallet jack, or steel shelving unit. The magnetic base holds firm under vibration and movement, but can be repositioned in seconds when operators need to move the tablet to a different piece of equipment.

The MagDock 360 is especially popular in operations where multiple workers share equipment across shifts. Instead of permanently mounting a tablet to one forklift, workers can carry their assigned device and magnetically dock it to whichever piece of equipment they are operating that shift.

XL Barcode Scanner Holder: The Industry's First Dedicated Mount

Warehouse workers frequently need both a tablet and a barcode scanner accessible from their forklift. The iBOLT XL Barcode Scanner Holder ($79.95) is a purpose-built cradle designed to hold full-size barcode scanners (including Honeywell, Zebra, and Symbol models) securely on the forklift alongside a mounted tablet.

The XL Scanner Holder uses the same 38mm C-size ball connection as the Bizmount, so it integrates directly into the same mounting infrastructure. Warehouse teams can install a tablet mount and a scanner holder side by side using iBOLT's dual-arm adapters, keeping both devices within easy reach of the operator.

Modular by Design

One of the core advantages of iBOLT's approach is modularity. The new forklift system uses the same industry-standard ball sizes (17mm, 20mm, 22mm, 25mm, 38mm, and 57mm) found across the iBOLT ecosystem of over 300 parts. A warehouse that installs the Bizmount Forklift base today can swap the tablet holder for a scanner holder, upgrade to a larger arm, or add a second device mount without replacing the base hardware. This modularity extends across iBOLT's full product line and is cross-compatible with RAM and other industry-standard mounting systems.

Real-World Durability Testing

iBOLT's engineering team tested the new forklift system in active warehouse environments over a six-month period prior to launch. The mounts were subjected to daily forklift operation across concrete, asphalt, and dock plate surfaces, with temperature exposure ranging from cold storage (35 degrees Fahrenheit) to non-climate-controlled summer warehouse conditions (110+ degrees Fahrenheit). The powder-coated steel showed no signs of corrosion, and the ball joints maintained their tension settings throughout the testing period.
